02 · Positioning & message

One sentence that says exactly who we're for.

For organizations doing fundable work who lack the time and federal fluency to win grants alone, Keystone is the advisory that runs the entire proposal lifecycle to the agency's exact spec — unlike freelancers or enterprise consultancies, because it's founder-built by someone who has done it, compliance-first, and within reach of first-time applicants.

Four message pillars
Pillar 01

We've sat where you sit

Founder-built credibility. Keystone was started by someone who wrote and submitted his own federal proposal — the authenticity competitors can't claim.

Pillar 02

Win on merit, not technicalities

The compliance-first promise. We make sure a format slip or missing form never costs you an award you earned.

Pillar 03

Idea to award, one partner

Full-cycle, not piecemeal. From the right solicitation to a verified submission — no handoffs, no gaps.

Pillar 04

Built for first-timers

Accessible expertise. The rigor of a big consultancy, scaled and priced for startups, labs, and small orgs.

03 · Go-to-market

Reach the beachhead where they already are.

First-time applicants cluster in known places — accelerators, SBDCs, LinkedIn, and the moment they register on SAM.gov. We meet them there with education first, pitch second.

Content pillars — earn trust before asking for it
Educate

How federal grants actually work

Plain-language guides: "SBIR in 10 minutes," solicitation breakdowns, the compliance traps that sink applications.

Prove

Case studies & outcomes

The representative engagements, made concrete — what changed, and why it mattered.

Alert

Open-opportunity radar

A recurring digest of newly-posted solicitations by sector — the reason to subscribe and stay.

Founder POV

Notes from the inside

The founder's firsthand take on navigating the federal system — the human thread through everything.

Channels & their job

Founder-led LinkedIn

The primary launch engine. Firsthand stories, grant breakdowns, and the build-in-public origin of Keystone — where the beachhead audience and referrers actually pay attention.

Awareness

Content & SEO

Evergreen guides that rank for "how to write an SBIR proposal" and similar intent searches — compounding inbound that works while you sleep.

Inbound

Partnerships

Co-marketing with incubators, accelerators, university tech-transfer offices, and SBDCs — they have the founders; we have the expertise they can't staff.

Distribution

Workshops & webinars

"SBIR 101" sessions hosted with partners — live proof of expertise that converts attendees into consultations.

Conversion

The Opportunity Radar newsletter

A sector-segmented digest of open solicitations and deadlines — the owned channel that keeps Keystone top-of-mind until the buying moment arrives.

04 · 90-day launch

Three phases from quiet to loud.

Phase 01

Foundation

Weeks −4 to 0 · before launch
Site & brand live — the charcoal identity, six service pages, founder story.
Seed content — 3–4 cornerstone guides published and indexable.
Warm the founder's LinkedIn — begin posting the build-in-public origin.
Line up 2–3 partners for launch-week workshops.
Phase 02

Launch

Weeks 1 to 4 · the moment
Announcement — founder-story post, launch film, press outreach in one wave.
First "SBIR 101" webinar with a partner audience.
Free readiness check — a low-friction offer to fill the consultation calendar.
Launch the Opportunity Radar and start the subscriber list.
Phase 03

Momentum

Months 2 to 6 · compounding
Publish real case studies as first engagements close.
Formalize partner referrals into a repeatable channel.
Test paid search against the highest-intent keywords.
Double down on whichever channel produced the best clients.

07 · News & PR

The exciting news — and how it travels.

A new firm earns attention with a story, not a press release. The founder's path is the hook; the launch and the free tool are the news pegs.

Announcement headlines — pick the strongest, A/B the rest
Founder angle

The builder who decoded the federal grant system — and turned it into a firm.

Leads with authenticity. Best for LinkedIn, founder communities, and podcasts.

Launch angle

Keystone launches to help startups and labs win federal funding they'd otherwise lose.

Clean, factual launch news. Best for trade press and partner channels.

Hook angle

Free grant-readiness check launches for first-time SBIR applicants.

A genuine news peg with a reason to act now. Best for newsletters and communities.
