The challenge

Reviewers reject for compliance long before they judge on merit.

A margin that’s too narrow, a font half a point too small, a missing biosketch, an ineligible cost — any one can move a proposal straight to the rejection pile, unread on the merits.

Compliance is the easiest points to keep and the easiest to lose. The rules are knowable and exact, but they’re scattered across the solicitation, the agency guide, and the forms — and they change between cycles.

Representative engagement

Cascade Materials was one formatting rule from disqualification.

Representative engagement
CM
Cascade Materials
Advanced materials · NSF proposal
The situation

Cascade, a materials-science team pursuing an NSF award, had written a strong proposal in-house and was a day from submitting. They asked us for a final compliance check “just to be safe.”

What we found

The cross-walk caught three issues that would have returned the proposal without review: the project description ran half a page over the limit, a required data-management plan was missing, and the biosketches used a retired format. None touched the science — all were fatal.

What we did

We flagged each with a fix, trimmed the overage without losing substance, supplied a compliant DMP template, and reformatted the biosketches to the current NSF version — then re-verified before sign-off.
