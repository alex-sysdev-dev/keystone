The challenge

Most wasted grant effort happens before the writing starts.

Teams pour months into a proposal for a program they were never eligible for, or one where their work is a poor thematic fit — and the rejection says nothing about the quality of their idea.

The federal landscape is vast and badly signposted. Thousands of active programs across 26 agencies, each with its own eligibility rules, set-asides, and unstated reviewer preferences. Choosing well is a research discipline, not a guess.

How it works

From a vague intention to a targeted decision.

01
Intake & objectives

A working session to understand your technology or program, your organization, your funding goal, and your real constraints on time and cost-share.

02
Landscape research

We build the shortlist — open solicitations and forecasted cycles — and verify eligibility against each before anything reaches you.

03
Scoring & comparison

Each candidate is scored on the same rubric so you can weigh a large, competitive award against a smaller, near-certain one with eyes open.

The situation

Before Keystone, founder Alexander Aguilar built AxiomOps — an agentic, multi-agent AI platform for logistics operations — and set out to fund it federally as a first-time applicant: no proposal experience, and a maze of programs to choose from.

What he learned

The highest-leverage decision wasn't the writing — it was the vehicle. Rather than force the platform into an ill-fitting grant, he targeted a USTRANSCOM Commercial Solutions Opening (AoI-902, logistics optimization) — a far better fit for a dual-use logistics tool.

What he did

He verified eligibility, registered the entity in SAM.gov and secured a UEI, decoded the solicitation, and wrote and submitted the proposal solo — the exact gauntlet this service now runs for you, so you don't face it alone.
